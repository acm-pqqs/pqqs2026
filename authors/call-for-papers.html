ACM Conference on Post-Quantum and Quantum-based Security

ACM PQQS 2026

November 2–4, 2026 • San Jose, CA, USA

Call for Papers

PQQS is a premier forum dedicated to research at the intersection of cybersecurity and quantum technologies.

The conference covers both quantum-enabled security systems and post-quantum cryptographic migration, integrating theory, systems, deployment, and standardization perspectives. We invite submissions describing original research contributions, experience reports, and system artifacts relevant to post-quantum and quantum-based security.

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

  • Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)
    • Algorithmic foundations: lattice-based, code-based, multivariate, hash-based, isogeny-based constructions
    • Security proofs and cryptanalysis of PQC schemes
    • Post-quantum key exchange, signatures, and authenticated encryption
    • Implementation security, side-channel resistance, and fault attacks
    • Hybrid cryptographic protocols and backward compatibility
    • Performance evaluation and optimization
    • Secure deployment in networks, cloud, IoT, cellular systems, and critical infrastructure
    • Migration strategies and cryptographic agility
    • Threat modeling under harvest-now-decrypt-later adversaries
    • Standardization and compliance (e.g., NIST PQC transition)
  • Quantum-Based Security
    • Quantum key distribution (QKD) and quantum communication networks
    • Security of quantum devices and hardware implementations
    • Security of quantum compilers and quantum cloud platforms
    • Verification and formal models for quantum protocols
    • Quantum-secure network architectures
    • Quantum randomness generation and certification
    • Side-channel and implementation attacks on quantum systems
    • Integration of classical and quantum cryptographic infrastructures
  • Systems and Infrastructure Security in the Quantum Era
    • Secure migration of large-scale distributed systems
    • Post-quantum security in 5G/6G, edge, and satellite networks
    • Quantum-safe PKI and identity management
    • Blockchain and distributed ledger security in a post-quantum world
    • Secure firmware, hardware accelerators, and trusted execution under PQ assumptions
    • Cloud and data center cryptographic transition
  • Applications, Policy, and Ecosystem Transition
    • Security risk assessment and transition planning
    • Economic and operational impacts of quantum migration
    • Certification, compliance, and regulatory frameworks
    • Government and industry migration case studies
    • Tools, benchmarks, and experimental platforms
